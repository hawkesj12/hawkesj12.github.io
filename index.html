What I've built

Atlas — a production AI operations platform

Live in production

A production AI operations platform at my current employer. Its agents retrieve and cite answers from the company's own documents, classify and route incoming work, and draft structured summaries; source material flows in through a deterministic ingestion pipeline. Model calls are cost-tiered — a cheap model handles extraction and classification, escalating to a stronger one only when a task needs it — and the whole system runs on Azure with managed identity and end-to-end tracing.

Why it matters: I originated the initiative and built it solo — from the architecture through the deployment — and delivered the executive demo. I don't just build it; I run and harden it in production.

Job-Radar ↗ — an applied-AI job-search engine

Open source

A self-growing engine that scans ~500 companies across the public hiring market — multiple ATS platforms and job aggregators, plus a Common Crawl bootstrap — scores every role against a weighted fit model, dedupes across sources, and (optionally) semantically re-ranks with an LLM. Config-driven and pip-installable, so anyone can point it at their own search.

Why it matters: I didn't just apply for AI-engineering jobs — I built the AI tooling to run the search, then open-sourced it. Data engineering, API integration, discovery-at-scale, and AI-driven matching in one system, shipped through three adversarial reviews.
